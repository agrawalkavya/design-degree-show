↯ Bottleneck anatomy
The overlock machine is not one bottleneck — it is a shadow line that runs parallel, at its own pace. When it stalls, every stage downstream stalls with it. It has no queue; it is the queue.
↯ Hidden time costs
Transportation is not a logistics decision — it is embedded in every stage. Fabric samples travel before fabric moves. Labels travel before garments are stitched. Transit time is production time, uncounted.
↯ Labour invisibility
Finishing is the most labour-dense stage and the least visible. Dhaaga cutting, accessory stitching, ironing — these operations hold no machine, no station name. They are absorbed into the per-piece rate and disappear.
↯ Waste typology
Waste is not only fabric at the blade — it is the end-of-roll remnant, the failed QC return, the over-procured accessory, the packaging discarded at dispatch. Every stage sheds something the order does not account for.
↯ The swatch book
The swatch book is the factory's memory. It resolves the distance between what a client describes and what exists in the store. Without it, the entire procurement cycle must repeat. It is a low-technology object holding the system together.
